Blockchain And Its Application To The Education Sector

Nov 15, 2017
By Admin
30 Comments

Wondering about what blockchain is and how can it be utilised in giant education sector? This article focuses on the potential of blockchain to create the next level of learning experiences for students as well as the education industry as a whole. As simple as said, if we use Blockchain Technology in Education Sector, it can very well do wonders for both the students and the tutors or examination cell alike.

Blockchain is a decentralised distributed ledger spread across many computers (nodes) without involving middlemen. And, every transaction on Blockchain is public, encrypted, synchronised ensuring transparency, security and nominal administration cost.

1. Hurdles of Online Education.

Handles the existing problems of online education. The biggest hurdles in any sort of online education is user retention and education completion rates. Blockchain technology in education sector offers solutions to these challenges. Further, the academic program can be tokenized to boost educational motivation. For example, if students are offered some amount of tokens on completion of online course, they will definitely be motivated to do so.On the other hand, it can also be used to motivate tutors and educators. For example, on uploading certain amount of tutorials and educational videos on platforms like YouTube and others, they can also be rewarded with tokens.

2. Highest Level of Security.

The benefits of Blockchain technology in education sector are not just limited to incentives. In fact, it also offers the biggest advantage of security. Security of storing credentials, storing online degrees and certificates, badges, etc. In addition, the cross border payments are also highly secured as well as trusted.

3. Utmost Transparency.

On the blockchain platform, since any created / added block needs approval of other involved parties, the chances are very rare of the record being fake or falsified. This approval criteria prevents students or educators from creating fake certifications to claim that the candidate has passed or excelled at a particular course.

4. Global Assessment

Our existing education system of certification is not an ideal fit. It is paper-based and hence subject to fraud and loss. At a global level, mobile population is increasing by leaps and bounds. Hence, a centralised database of academic achievements and individual credentials definitely makes sense. Here, the blockchain technology can lend a helping hand. In this decentralised ledger, records pertaining to achievements can be stored. These records can be assessed by anyone at a global level. For students or employees, moving out of native country to a foreign land, this can be of great help. Universities or recruiters of other country can easily verify details and credentials.
